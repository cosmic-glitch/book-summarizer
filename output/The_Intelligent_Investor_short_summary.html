The Intelligent Investor: Key Ideas

Investment vs Speculation

  • Investment promises safety of principal and adequate return, while speculation does not
  • Defensive investors should have a 25-75% allocation between stocks and bonds, rebalancing when needed
  • Aggressive investors should look for undervalued securities and special situations

Impacts of Inflation

  • Inflation erodes bond returns but stocks provide some protection against rising prices
  • Conservative investors need a balance of both stocks and bonds to minimize risks from unexpected inflation or deflation

Market Fluctuations

  • Most investors cannot successfully time the market, so a formulaic 50/50 stocks/bonds approach is prudent
  • True investors are owners of companies, not just stock traders, and should not be overly swayed by short-term fluctuations
  • Buy when prices are irrationally low and sell when irrationally high, as demonstrated by the "Mr. Market" analogy

Defensive Stock Selection

  • Look for companies with:
    • Large size
    • Strong finances
    • Earnings and dividend stability
    • Moderate valuations and growth rates
  • A quantitative, criteria-based approach can yield a diversified portfolio of high-quality, reasonably priced stocks

Margin of Safety

  • Defined as the gap between price and estimated intrinsic value, which allows room for error and misfortune
  • Is the central concept that distinguishes investment from speculation
  • Is harder to find in the current high-priced market, especially for growth stocks
  • Undervalued, "bargain" securities can have a large margin of safety despite being viewed as speculative
