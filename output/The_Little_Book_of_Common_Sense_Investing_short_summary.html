The Little Book of Common Sense Investing

Key Ideas

  • The Power of Index Investing
    • Over the long run, individual investors are better off owning low-cost index funds that track the entire stock market rather than trying to pick winning stocks or actively managed mutual funds.
    • Index funds have significantly lower costs, are more tax-efficient, and deliver higher returns than the vast majority of actively managed funds.
  • The Relentless Rules of Humble Arithmetic
    • The high costs of investing, including expense ratios, transaction costs, sales loads, and taxes, dramatically reduce the returns earned by the average investor.
    • Minimizing these costs is the most reliable way to maximize long-term investment returns. Index funds keep costs to a bare minimum.
  • The Importance of Asset Allocation
    • An investor's allocation between stocks and bonds is a crucial decision that has a major impact on long-term returns and risk.
    • A simple balanced index fund with 60% stocks and 40% bonds is an excellent choice for most investors, providing strong returns and reasonable stability.
  • Staying the Course
    • Once an investor has selected a prudent asset allocation and low-cost index funds, the most important thing is to stay the course and maintain a long-term perspective.
    • Avoid chasing past performance, attempting to time the market, or letting emotions drive investment decisions. The winning strategy is to buy and hold for the long run.
  • Focus on the Wisdom of Great Investors
    • Many of the greatest investors in history, from Benjamin Graham to Warren Buffett, have advised that index funds are the best way for most people to invest in stocks.
    • Their commonsense principles - minimize costs, diversify widely, invest for the long-term - are accessible to everyone through index funds.

By keeping costs low and harnessing the long-term growth of business and the stock market, investors can secure their financial futures and, in the words of Benjamin Franklin, find "the way to wealth."
